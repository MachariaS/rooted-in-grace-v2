There is a moment in many relationships when we quietly wonder, *"What changed?"*

The conversations aren't as effortless. The excitement feels distant. The little things that once made us smile slowly become things we overlook.

It's tempting to conclude that love has died.

But has it?

Or has it simply grown quiet beneath the weight of busy schedules, unmet expectations, unspoken disappointments, and the ordinary pressures of life?

Love rarely disappears all at once.

More often, it fades in the places we stop tending. We become familiar with one another. We assume people know they're appreciated. We postpone difficult conversations. We choose being right over understanding.

Slowly, distance replaces closeness.

The beautiful thing about love is that it isn't sustained by feelings alone. It grows through intentionality. Through choosing kindness when it's easier to withdraw. Through listening when we'd rather defend ourselves. Through making time when life insists we're too busy.

Not every relationship is meant to last forever. Some seasons end, and some people are only part of our journey for a time.

But before we say, *"The love is gone,"* perhaps we should ask a gentler question:

*"Have we stopped nurturing what once came so naturally?"*

Whether it's a friendship, a relationship, a marriage, or even the way we relate to our families; love flourishes where it is cared for.

Perhaps today is an invitation to send the message you've been postponing. To have the honest conversation you've been avoiding. To forgive. To apologise. To make time.

Because sometimes, love isn't asking to be replaced.

It's asking to be revived.
