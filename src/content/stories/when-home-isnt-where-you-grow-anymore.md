I met someone who had attended the same church for as long as they could remember.

Every Sunday felt familiar. They knew every song, every face, and every corner of the sanctuary. It was the church where they had learned to pray, where they had memorized Bible verses as a child, and where their parents still faithfully worshipped.

Then their twenties arrived.

They found themselves visiting other churches.

Some had vibrant worship; others offered deeper Bible teaching. Some had thriving communities of young adults who understood the questions they were carrying. For the first time, they wondered whether staying was loyalty; or simply habit.

The questions became impossible to ignore.

*"Am I betraying my family if I leave?"*

*"Am I chasing excitement instead of conviction?"*

*"Or is God inviting me into a season of growth?"*

One day, an older believer shared a simple thought.

*"Don't choose a church because it entertains you. Don't stay in one simply because it's familiar. Choose the place where you're continually becoming more like Christ."*

Those words stayed with them.

They realized the decision had never really been about buildings, music, or denominations.

It was about discipleship.

A healthy church won't simply make you feel welcome; it will teach you the Scriptures, encourage accountability, lovingly correct you, and continually point you back to Jesus, even when it feels uncomfortable.

Sometimes, that church is the one your parents introduced you to.

Sometimes, it isn't.

Neither decision should be made carelessly.

The goal isn't to find the perfect church.

The goal is to remain planted where your faith is nurtured; where your gifts can serve others; and where Christ remains at the center of everything.

Because at the end of the day, the church isn't the destination.

Jesus is.
