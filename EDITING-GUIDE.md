# Editing Rooted in Grace — A Guide for You

No coding, no technical setup — everything below happens in your web browser.

---

## Logging in

1. Go to **yoursite.netlify.app/admin/** (use your actual site address).
2. Log in with the email and password you set up when you got your invite.
3. You'll land on a screen with a list on the left: Stories, Podcast
   Episodes, Journal Posts, Grace Notes, Events, and Site Settings. That's
   everything on the site you can change.

If you ever forget your password, click "Forgot password?" on the login screen.

---

## How publishing works here

When you save something, it doesn't go live immediately — it goes into a
little holding area first, so you always get a chance to double check
before the world sees it. Every item you're working on shows a colored
status at the top:

- **Draft** — you're still working on it, nobody else can see it
- **In Review** — you've marked it ready; a good moment to re-read it
- **Ready** — click **Publish** and it goes live on the site within a
  couple of minutes

You can always come back and change something later, even after it's published.

## Publishing a story

1. Click **Stories** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **New Story** (top right).
3. Fill in:
   - **Title** — the story's headline
   - **Excerpt** — one or two sentences that appear on the story preview card
   - **Cover Image** — optional; click the box to upload a photo
   - **Cover Image Alt Text** — a short description of the photo, for
     people using screen readers (e.g. "A woman sitting on a park bench at sunrise")
   - **Author Name** — leave this blank and turn on **Publish Anonymously**
     if the person doesn't want their name shown
   - **Publish Date** — when it should be dated
   - **Feature on Homepage** — turn this on if you want it to be the big
     featured story on the homepage
   - **Story** — the full story goes here. You can bold text, add
     headings, and add links using the toolbar above the box.
4. Click **Save** in the top right.
5. When you're happy with it, change the status to **Ready** and click
   **Publish**.

## Adding an event

1. Click **Events** → **New Event**.
2. Fill in the title, description, and date & time.
3. Choose **Format**: Online or In person.
4. **Location or Meeting Link** — paste your Zoom link, or the street
   address, whichever applies.
5. **Registration URL** — if you're using Eventbrite, a Google Form, or
   similar, paste the link here. If you leave this blank, the site will
   show a "Get notified" button instead that points people to Grace Notes.
6. **Capacity Note** — optional, e.g. "Limited to 20 people."
7. Save and publish, same as above.

**You don't need to do anything when the event date passes.** The site
automatically moves it from "Upcoming" to "Past Gatherings" on its own —
though this happens the next time the site rebuilds, which is triggered by
any publish, usually within a day.

## Marking a past event's recording

Once an event has happened and you have a replay link (say, a YouTube
recording or a Zoom cloud recording link):

1. Click **Events**, then open the event that already happened.
2. Scroll to **Recording URL** and paste the link.
3. If you have photos from the gathering, scroll to **Photo Gallery** and
   click **Add Photo** to upload as many as you like.
4. Save and publish. The recording link and photos will now show up on
   that event's card under Past Gatherings.

## Updating the community link

If your WhatsApp group, Discord, or wherever your community lives changes
its invite link:

1. Click **Site Settings** in the sidebar.
2. Click **General Settings**.
3. Find **Community Join URL** and replace it with the new link.
4. Save and publish. Every "Join Us" button across the whole site updates
   automatically — you only have to change it in this one place.

This same Site Settings page is also where you update your podcast
platform links, social media links, contact email, and the tagline at the
top of the homepage.

## A few other things worth knowing

- **Grace Notes** are your short reflections — same process as a story,
  just shorter, under **Grace Notes** in the sidebar. These also appear as
  an archive for visitors to browse.
- **Journal Posts** need a **Category** (Hope, Healing, Faith, Community,
  or Wellness) so they show up in the right filter on the Journal page.
- **Podcast Episodes**: for the Spotify link, open your episode in Spotify,
  click **Share → Embed episode**, and copy the address inside `src="..."`
  from the code it gives you — that's the "Spotify Embed URL" field.
- **Sample entries**: right now, each section has one example item marked
  "This Is a Sample Entry," so the site doesn't look empty. Once you've
  published a couple of real ones, feel free to delete the sample or just
  leave it — it's clearly marked and does no harm.
- **Deleting something**: open the item, and look for the trash/delete
  option near the Save button.
- If anything looks wrong on the live site after you publish, give it a
  minute or two — changes take a short moment to go live, they're not
  instant.

That's everything. If you get stuck, the worst that can happen is you save
a draft that isn't quite right — nothing you do here can break the site.
